Dear Edgars,

When I was interning at Swedbank's Credit Management team in 2024, I noticed something that bothered me: specialists were spending up to eight hours a week on tasks that did not require human judgement. I initiated a process improvement project to automate those hours using RPA and AI. That instinct — to spot an inefficiency and move from observation to action — is precisely what the Area Manager role calls for, and it is what drives me to apply today.

Since then I have been working as a Management Consultant in Strategy & Growth at Civitta, where AI is not a side interest but a daily operating mode. I use Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT, and Perplexity to run complex financial regressions, build forecasting models, structure market research, and automate repetitive workflows. I built a financial literacy investing game — MarketCycle — end-to-end through vibe-coding with Claude Code, which gave me a direct appreciation for what it takes to translate a business idea into a working product without a deep engineering background. That is the same translation challenge sitting at the heart of this role: converting corporate financing workflow gaps into concrete AI and RPA solutions.

The role asks for someone who can analyse processes, identify where AI creates measurable value, build the business case, own implementation, and drive adoption. My Swedbank internship in Credit Management — working directly under Aigar Agu, Baltic Head of Credit Management — gave me first-hand exposure to credit processes, risk workflows, and where they break down at scale. That grounding in how banking operations actually function is complemented by a rigorous academic foundation: as a Teaching Assistant for SSE Riga's flagship finance courses, Financial Economics and Management Accounting and Finance, I have taught undergraduates the key corporate finance principles that directly align with banking. My consulting work has sharpened my ability to structure problems, build KPI frameworks, and communicate complex ideas to senior stakeholders in plain language. I use AI workflows daily, which allows me to understand what can be optimised with AI and what cannot.

The Baltic dimension of the role matters to me specifically. I am Estonian by background, studied at SSE Riga, and work across Nordic and Baltic projects currently. Cross-border collaboration is not unfamiliar territory; it is where I operate naturally.

Sincerely,

Rasmus Riim
